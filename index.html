2020 Computing Innovation Fellow postdoc at the University of Pennsylvania

How to say Lara: /'lɑɹ.ə/

I'm on the faculty job market fall 2022!

I teach computers how to tell stories (also known as automated story generation).
More generally, I do Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence with a focus on natural language. I look at how humans communicate and design/create artificially intelligent agents to leverage that.
Professionally interested in cognition and computational creativity; personally interested in AI equity and science communication.
My pronouns are she/they.

Brief Bio

Third-Person Bio

Dr. Lara J. Martin is a 2020 Computing Innovation Fellow (CIFellow) postdoctoral researcher at the University of Pennsylvania working with Dr. Chris Callison-Burch. In 2020, she earned her PhD in Human-Centered Computing from the Georgia Institute of Technology, where she worked with Dr. Mark Riedl. She also has a MS in Language Technologies from Carnegie Mellon University and a BS in Computer Science & Linguistics from Rutgers University—New Brunswick. Dr. Martin’s work resides in the field of Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence with a focus on natural language applications. They have worked in the areas of automated story generation, speech processing, and affective computing, publishing in top-tier conferences such as AAAI and IJCAI. They have also been featured in Wired and BBC Science Focus magazine.

About Me

My research interests include:
  • computational creativity,
  • interactive narrative and narrative generation & understanding,
  • augmentative alternative communication (AAC),
  • prosody & emotion (affective computing),
  • dialog & conversational agents,
  • and cognitive systems.

One of my non-research passions is in educating children about computer science and technology — especially getting young girls and other minorities in tech interested in tech, and I try to find as many local outreach opportunities as I can.

I have a Tech to Teaching certificate from Georgia Tech. I have taught as sole instructor of record for an undergraduate Intro to Cognitive Science class and have co-taught a graduate course on Interactive Fiction and Text Generation with Chris Callison-Burch.

I enjoy playing computer games and board games, petting dogs, hiking, SCUBA diving, playing piano and accordion, collecting accents, and doing random craft projects. And I'm married to Dr. David Kent, the best person.
